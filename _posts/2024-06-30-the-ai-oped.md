## About this paper

This essay was written for [EXPO 15: Fundamentals of Academic Writing](https://coursebrowser.dce.harvard.edu/course/fundamentals-of-academic-writing-15/) at Harvard, a course devoted to the mechanics of academic argument: formulating a defensible thesis, marshaling evidence, anticipating counterarguments, and revising through successive drafts and peer workshops. The assignment took the form of an op-ed — a genre that demands the same rigor as a scholarly essay while imposing the additional disciplines of brevity, urgency, and a public audience.

The essay addresses the public debate around artificial intelligence in the op-ed form. Writing it was as much an exercise in *how* to argue — earning each claim before extending it — as in *what* to argue; its central claim will be presented in the excerpt below once the final text is uploaded.
