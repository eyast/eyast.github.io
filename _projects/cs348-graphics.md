## Overview

This page collects selected results from my computer graphics coursework (CS348), produced in SideFX Houdini and rendered with the Karma renderer. The exercises span the full production path — procedural modeling, look development, character animation, and secondary simulation — and two studies are highlighted below: a stylized *running man* and a *chicken* study that progresses from a static turntable to behavioral animation with particulate simulation.

## The Running Man

The first study animates a humanoid figure in a full-body run cycle. The emphasis is on the interplay between primary motion (the skeletal run) and procedurally layered secondary motion, timed and rendered to convey weight and urgency.

## The Chicken

The second study develops a single character asset through three stages of increasing complexity. A turntable render establishes the model and its materials; a behavioral pass animates the bird pecking and investigating its surroundings; and a final pass couples the animation with a dust simulation, so that the character's motion excites — and is grounded by — its environment.
